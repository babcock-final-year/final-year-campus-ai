## Overview

The **Convocation Ceremony** is the flagship event of the Babcock University academic year — a formal gathering at which degrees, diplomas, and honorary doctorates are conferred. It is held annually in **July**, and is preceded by a full **Graduation Week** of events spanning 8 days.

The ceremony is jointly held for **undergraduate** and **postgraduate** students, though on separate days.

## Standard Graduation Week Events (All Years)

| Event | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Departmental Inductions** | School/professional body inductions held during graduation week (Nursing, MLS, Education/TRCN, Law, etc.) |
| **PG Meet & Greet / Cocktail Party** | Postgraduate social networking event; typically at BBS |
| **PCF Banquet / Luncheon** | PCF-organised banquet for graduating students (Queen Esther Activity Hall) |
| **Alumni Connectivity — A Day with the Stars** | Amphitheater event connecting graduates with alumni mentors and industry leaders |
| **Grand Graduation Banquet** | Joint PG and UG formal dinner at BU Stadium |
| **Convocation Lecture** | Academic lecture by a distinguished guest or university appointee; open to the public |
| **Doctoral Hooding Ceremony** | Separate ceremony for PhD graduates; held at the Amphitheater |
| **Dress Rehearsal** | Mandatory rehearsal at BU Stadium for all graduating students |
| **Tree Planting** | Graduating class plants trees on main campus and Iperu campus as a legacy gift |
| **Project Dedication** | Class dedication of a physical project (infrastructure/community contribution) to the university |
| **Consecration Service** | Spiritual service the night before Convocation; held at the Amphitheater |
| **Baccalaureate Service** | Sabbath morning worship service for graduates and families |
| **Vesper Service** | Sabbath evening worship |
| **Convocation Ceremony** | Grand finale; degrees conferred by the Chancellor and Pro-Chancellor; held Sunday morning |
| **BUGC Meeting** | Graduating class executives meeting (BGH Conference Room) |

## Convocation Ceremony — Structure

1. Academic procession in full regalia
2. Opening prayer
3. National Anthem and University Anthem
4. Presentation of candidates by their respective Deans
5. Conferment of degrees by the Chancellor / Pro-Chancellor
6. Award of prizes (Best Graduating Student, Faculty Bests, etc.)
7. Conferment of Honorary Degrees (where applicable)
8. VC's charge to graduates
9. Recessional

## Graduation Gowns — Rules

- Academic gowns are **mandatory** for the Convocation ceremony
- Gowns are provided by the university / BUGC and collected before the ceremony
- Gowns must be **returned to the designated collection centre on the same day** before leaving campus
- Students who wish to remain on campus after Matriculation/Convocation may stay until the stated deadline (e.g., Wednesday after the event); all students must vacate halls by the final deadline
