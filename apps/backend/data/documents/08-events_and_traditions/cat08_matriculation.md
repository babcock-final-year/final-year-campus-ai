## Overview

The **Undergraduate Matriculation Ceremony** is the formal event at which newly admitted 100-level and Direct Entry students are officially inducted as students of Babcock University. It is the symbolic first step in their academic journey, and the VC typically addresses matriculants with the observation: *"The joy of being matriculated is that you know exactly when you are graduating."*

## Matriculation Process

1. Students must be **financially registered** before they can matriculate
2. A **Matriculation Form** is issued and completed by eligible students
3. Students take a formal **Oath of Matriculation** — pledging to abide by university rules
4. The VC and SVP Academic Affairs address the new students
5. Parents and guardians are invited to attend
6. After the ceremony, students are officially recognised as full members of the university

---

## Post-Matriculation Rules (Important)

- Students must **return gowns** to the designated collection centre on the same day
- Students who wish to remain on campus after the ceremony may do so until the stated date (e.g., Wednesday following the ceremony)
- **All students must vacate halls by the published final deadline** (e.g., Thursday after the ceremony for the 26th Matriculation)
- New Year break begins immediately after the ceremony for 100L students
- Resumption date for the next semester is communicated at a later time

## Significance

Matriculation is one of BU's most significant traditions. The VC's annual address at matriculation emphasises:
- The diversity of students from across Africa and beyond
- The importance of good character alongside academic excellence
- Warning against cultism, hooliganism, and misconduct
- The university's commitment to quality education within a Christian framework
