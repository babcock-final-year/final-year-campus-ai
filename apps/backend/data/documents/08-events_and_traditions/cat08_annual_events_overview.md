## Overview

Babcock University observes a rich annual calendar of academic, spiritual, and social events. These events are deeply tied to the university's Seventh-day Adventist identity and its academic mission. Most major events recur every session with fixed patterns, though specific dates change each year.

## Founder's Day — September 17

Founder's Day commemorates the formal charter of Babcock University on **May 10, 1999**, but the annual celebration is held on **September 17** each session (the start of the academic year). The event includes:
- Special chapel service / thanksgiving service
- VC address to staff and students
- Recognition of long-serving staff
- Cultural displays and student activities

> Historical note: The university traces its roots to 1959 as ACWA; it was chartered as a degree-granting university in 1999, making it one of Nigeria's first three private universities.

## Week of Spiritual Emphasis

A **university-wide spiritual emphasis week** is held every Second Semester:
- All academic lectures and normal campus activities are paused during this week
- A guest speaker (typically a pastor or evangelist) leads daily services
- Morning devotions, midday services, and evening meetings are held across worship centres
- 2025/2026 dates: **Saturday, February 7 – Saturday, February 14, 2026**

---

## Inaugural Lectures

Inaugural lectures are formal academic events at which a newly promoted or appointed professor delivers a public lecture on their area of research. Key details:
- Held throughout the session (dates communicated by RIIC/Registry)
- Venue: **BBS Main Auditorium**, Babcock Business School building
- Open to staff, students, and the public
- BU has delivered **59+ inaugural lectures** as of the 2025/2026 session
- Featured in university news archives at legacy.babcock.edu.ng/news

## Career & Job Fair Week

- Held **mid-March** each session
- **Lecture-free** for graduating (final-year) students
- Organised by the Babcock Entrepreneurship Development Centre (BEDC) and Career Services
- Companies and organisations visit campus for recruitment talks, interviews, and networking
- 2025/2026 dates: **March 16–20, 2026**

## Research Fair

- Organised by the **Office of Research, Innovation and International Cooperation (RIIC)**
- Open to the entire university community

The **Nigeria Association of Adventist Students (NAAS)** holds an annual campus week:

## Babcock Entrepreneurship Week (BEW)

- Hosted by **BEDC (Babcock Entrepreneurship Development Centre)**

## Babcock Innovation Challenge (BIC)

- BEDC's flagship annual student startup competition
- Operates throughout the session with a Grand Finale in November
- 2025/2026 Grand Finale: **Sunday, November 23, 2025**
- Top student innovators pitch to judges for prizes and incubation support

## Congregation

- A formal ceremony where eligible graduating students are recognised
- Distinct from Convocation: Congregation is typically a smaller, semester-specific event
- 2025/2026 Congregation: **Thursday, November 20, 2025**
