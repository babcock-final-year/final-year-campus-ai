## Inter-University Transfer Admission

Babcock University **does not normally accept transfer students** due to the exigent nature of its programmes. However, students wishing to transfer from other accredited institutions may apply under the following conditions:

### Requirements for Transfer Applicants
- Follow the **same application procedure** as new applicants
- Submit **complete official transcripts** of all studies taken at the previous institution (even if no credit was earned)
- Submit a **letter of recommendation** from the current institution
- Present a **JAMB Admission Letter**, **Official Transcript**, and an **Attestation Letter** from the previous university, sent **directly** from that university to: **The Registrar, Babcock University, Ilishan-Remo, Ogun State** or emailed to **registrar@babcock.edu.ng**
- Effect the inter-university transfer with JAMB

### Credit Transfer Rules
- Only courses **comparable to Babcock University courses** and passed with a minimum grade of **C** (2.0 on a 4-point scale OR 2.5 on a 5-point scale) will be accepted
- The overall CGPA on all previous college work must be at least **C**
- A maximum of **84 semester hours** may be accepted from a college where the highest degree offered is an associate degree
- Transferred credits **will not appear on Babcock-issued transcripts** if the student requests a transcript before graduation
- Transfer credits **will not be used** in CGPA calculations
- Senate approval is required for a registered student to take classes at another institution

### Residency Requirement for Transfer Students
- Must spend a **minimum of 3 years in residence** at Babcock before graduation
- Must complete **all GEDS (General Education) courses** at Babcock regardless of prior credits
- Background deficiencies identified in transcripts or entrance exams will be addressed individually

### Ineligibility
A student who was **dismissed or is on probation** from another institution for poor scholarship or citizenship **may not be eligible** for transfer admission.

## International Student Admissions

### Eligibility
An international student must have obtained the **equivalent of the Nigerian Senior Secondary School Certificate (SSSC)**. The student must:
- Provide **original transcripts, records, and official copies** of all credits, degrees, diplomas, and credentials — validated by faculty or national officials
- Provide documents **in the original language** plus a **certified English translation** (validated at the country's embassy, if originally in another language)
- Demonstrate **English proficiency** (written and oral) before regular admission is offered
- Have or obtain a **student residence/study permit or visa** before studying at Babcock

### Application Process
1. Submit application with all required credentials to the Registrar for evaluation
2. Remain in home country until an **official letter of admission** is received
   - This letter is only issued if: academic credentials are satisfactory, a **surety advance deposit** has been made, and the student can demonstrate financial ability to fund their education
3. Upon arrival, report to the **International Student's Adviser** at the Registry with all documents

### Support Services
The **Student Support Centre (SSC)** provides dedicated support to international students, including:
- Adjustment to rules, regulations, and socio-cultural acclimatisation
- Academic counselling
- Special needs services
- Parent/guardian communication

## Readmission for Suspended Students

Students whose suspension period is about to end and wish to return must:
1. Apply **at least one month before** the end of their suspension period
2. Provide testimonials from parents/guardians, employers/supervisors, and a counsellor/clergy
3. If approved for readmission:
   - Begin registration at the **Student Support Centre**
   - Undergo a **3-month Special Rehabilitation Programme** during the first semester upon return (at the student's own expense)
