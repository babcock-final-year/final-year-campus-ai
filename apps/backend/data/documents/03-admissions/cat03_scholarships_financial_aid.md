## Overview

A limited number of financial aid programmes are available to deserving Babcock University students. Awards vary according to financial need and availability of funds. Most were established by friends of the university, former staff, and alumni.

**Where to apply:** Office of the Registrar — application forms for all scholarships and bursaries are available there.
**Contact for financial assistance eligibility:** Student Financial Aid Officer, Office of the SVP/DVC Academics

---

## General Eligibility Rules

To qualify for any scholarship or bursary:
- Must be a **full-time registered student** at Babcock University
- Must meet the **specific requirements** listed under each award
- Must carry a **full academic load** (normally 16 credit points per semester)
- Continuance is **contingent** on satisfactory academic progress, citizenship record, and class attendance each semester
- Students **may not combine** two scholarships or bursary awards
- Awards **cannot be redeemed for cash**, in whole or in part
- **Most scholarships have closing dates** — late applications are not normally considered
- If a recipient voluntarily drops out or faces disciplinary action within the semester, they must **refund** the cost of studies for that semester (the award is deemed relinquished)

## Work-Study Programme

The **Work-Study Scholarship** allows students to earn part of their tuition through on-campus employment.

### Key Details
- A student may earn **up to 20%** of their tuition through full involvement in the work-study programme during the school year
- Part-time work assignments are made at the **start of each semester**, aligned with class schedules
- Resident students are expected to work **10–20 hours per week**
- Earnings may be credited to the student account or withdrawn monthly per policy
- Suspension or dismissal from any campus employment automatically **debars the student** from all other work opportunities

### Work Departments
Bakery · Peanut Butter Factory · Wood Workshop · Food Services · Administrative Offices · Farm · Other service areas

**Contact:** Human Resources Department

## Student Literature Evangelist Scholarship Plan

One of the most significant sources of student financial aid at Babcock. Students participate in **literature evangelism** (May–August summer programme, and optionally during the academic year) and earn scholarship credits.

Full details available from the nearest SDA Conference office or the **VP for Student Development and Services**.

## Family Discount

A **5% discount** on the net tuition and residential fees is available to each full-time student who is a **dependent child** in a family with **two or more children** concurrently enrolled full-time at Babcock University.

### Eligibility for "Dependent" Status
- Not married
- Not in a de facto relationship
- Not in receipt of regular remuneration
- Under age 21 at the commencement of studies at Babcock

## Health Service Coverage

- Routine treatments, minor surgery, and prescription medications are covered by student accident/medical insurance up to **₦25,000 per session**
- Charges beyond this ceiling must be paid by the student
- Married students are responsible for the medical care and expenses of their family members
