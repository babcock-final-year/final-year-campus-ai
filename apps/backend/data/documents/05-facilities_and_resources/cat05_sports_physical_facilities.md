## Babcock Sports Complex

The **Babcock University Sports Complex** was built by the **Parents Consultative Forum** in **2009** as a major development gift to the university. It is of **international standard** with a capacity exceeding **10,000 persons**.

### Facilities Within the Sports Complex

| Facility | Details |
|----------|---------|
| Indoor Sports Pavilion | Multi-purpose indoor arena for indoor sports |
| Changing Rooms | Separate changing and rest rooms for athletes |
| Administrative Offices | Sports management offices |
| Fitness Centre | Fully equipped gym with modern fitness equipment |

### Sports Programme
The Sports Centre (under the Division of Management Services) coordinates all sports activities. Sports at Babcock are oriented toward **intramural competition** and total personal development rather than competitive inter-university rivalry.

**Organised sports include:**
- Football (Soccer)
- Basketball
- Volleyball
- Racquet sports (Squash, Badminton, Tennis)
- Cross-Country Race / Road Walk
- Annual Inter-Faculty Games
- Inter-Faculty Athletic Competition
- Fitness and endurance activities
- Sports club formations (Golf, Squash, Volleyball, Basketball, etc.)
- Coaching clinics

### Multi-Purpose Use
The Sports Complex also serves as venue for:
- Graduation / Convocation Ceremonies
- Week of Spiritual Emphasis
- Career Week for graduating students
- Large-scale university events

## Academic Buildings (Key Facilities)

### Babcock Business School (BBS) Building
- A ₦900 million project completed in **June 2012**
- Rebranded the **School of Management and Social Sciences**
- Features:
  - Smart, air-conditioned classrooms
  - Seminar rooms and lecture theatres
  - Multiple auditoriums (Auditorium A, B, C; Class 102)
  - Iconic fountain plaza: 17 water outlets with light effects; centre spray reaches 3 metres

### Education and Humanities (EAH) Building
- Houses the School of Education and Humanities
- Location of **Hope FM 89.1** radio station
- EAH 1 and EAH 2 branch libraries located here

### FAD Hall (Felicia Adebisi Dada Hall)
- Female student hall on the main campus (100–200L)
- Also houses **New Creation Chapel** auditorium

## Ellen G. White & SDA Research Centre

Established in **1990**, this is an Adventist heritage collection of Spirit of Prophecy materials — published and unpublished — released by the Ellen G. White Estate. It is located on the main campus and serves as a scholarly resource for:
- Denominational history research
- Adventist theology and inspiration studies
- Workshops and seminars on SDA heritage
- Reproduction of research materials at affordable costs for the West-Central Africa Division

## Media Facilities

### Hope FM 89.1
- University campus/community radio station
- Broadcasting licence secured in **2009**; commissioned **May 31, 2012**
- Transmits on **89.1 MHz** to both campuses and neighbouring communities
- Also accessible via **internet streaming**
- Primary practical training hub for Mass Communication students (radio writing and presentation)
- Programming includes music, news, religious shows, health content, and interviews
- Housed in the **Education and Humanities (EAH) Building**; mainly student-operated

### Adventist World Radio (AWR) Nigeria Studio
- Located in the annex of the University Church building
- Produces **1,456 thirty-minute programmes per year** in Hausa, Igbo, Yoruba, and Pidgin
- Broadcasts on the **shortwave (SW) Band**
- Fully soundproofed with computerised professional recording and production equipment
- Open to commercial clients for gospel-aligned music, drama, and jingle production

### WAD Media Centre
- Set up in **2019** in partnership with **Hope Channel International** (SDA satellite TV arm)
- Ultra-modern **television production studio** on campus
- Serves as the primary TV production hub for Mass Communication students
- Programming covers spiritual, health, children, and family life content via Facebook, YouTube, and Hope Channel International satellite

### Babcock University Schools (Feeder Institutions)
- **Babcock University Staff School** — primary school for staff children and community
- **Babcock University High School** — secondary school; strong national examination results; autonomous with self-balancing budget
