## Babcock University Teaching Hospital (BUTH)

The **Babcock University Teaching Hospital (BUTH)** is an ultra-modern tertiary health facility serving students, staff, and the wider community. It is one of the most advanced private teaching hospitals in Nigeria.

### Key Facilities & Features

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Total bed capacity | 140 beds+ |
| Cardiac Centre | Specialises in minor and major heart surgeries; over **300 open-heart surgeries** performed with **100% success rate** since 2015 |
| Radio Diagnostic Laboratory | Well-equipped diagnostic imaging unit |
| Medical College Complex | Houses research and teaching laboratories, skill acquisition labs, and administrative offices for the Benjamin S. Carson College of Health and Medical Sciences |
| Auditorium | 600-seater auditorium (also used as **Heir of the Kingdom Chapel**) |
| Worship Centres | Siloam Valley Church and Heir of the Kingdom Chapel are both located at BUTH |

### Services
- Diagnosis and treatment of general medical conditions
- Cardiac care (including open-heart surgery)
- Radiological diagnostics
- Clinical training for Medicine, Nursing, Allied Health, and related programmes
- Emergency medical services on campus

### BUTH Transportation Support
The Security Department assists BUTH in transporting sick students and staff from halls/homes to and from the hospital.

### Medical Insurance
All students are covered by a university health insurance scheme of **₦25,000 per session** (included in the fees structure — verify current amount before embedding).

## Student Health Centre (Campus Clinic)

In addition to BUTH, a **campus health centre / clinic** is available to students for routine medical care and first aid on the main Ilishan-Remo campus.

- Sick students who are medically approved may be excused from worship and public gatherings with a written permit from the Hall Administrator (countersigned by the health centre)
- Medical students must obtain authorisation signed at BUTH for clinical placements and off-campus medical activities (ref: Undergraduate Bulletin, BUTH authorisation policy)
