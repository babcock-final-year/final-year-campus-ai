## Overview

Babcock University is governed through a layered structure of ecclesiastical ownership, a governing council, university senate, and administrative leadership. Being a Seventh-day Adventist institution, the church forms the ultimate proprietor, but day-to-day governance is carried out by university leadership.

---

## Proprietor

**Seventh-day Adventist Church** — Specifically, the **West-Central Africa Division** and the three Union Conferences in Nigeria (Western, Eastern, and Northern Nigeria Union Conferences).

---

## University Council

The University Council is the governing body of Babcock University. It provides oversight on policy, finances, and the general direction of the institution. The inaugural Chairman of the University Council was **Pastor Luka T. Daniel**, President of the Africa-Indian Ocean Division of Seventh-day Adventists, who received the Certificate of Registration in 1999.


---

## University Senate

The **University Senate** has responsibility for and authority over all academic content, policies, and information. Every step of the university's educational process — from admission through graduation — receives appropriate approval by University officials and academic committees. The Senate approves programme changes, academic regulations, and degree requirements.

---

## Office of the Vice-Chancellor / President

The **Vice-Chancellor (VC)** also carries the title of **President** and serves as the chief executive officer of the university. The VC provides overall academic and administrative leadership.

**Current/Recent VC:** Prof. Afolarin Olutunde Ojewole (assumed office December 2025)

## Colleges and Schools

Babcock University is organised into one college and multiple schools:

### Benjamin S. Carson Sr. College of Health and Medical Sciences
- School of Basic Medical Sciences
- School of Basic Clinical Sciences
- School of Clinical Sciences
- School of Nursing Science
- School of Public and Allied Health

### Other Schools (Main Campus — Ilishan-Remo)
- School of Computing
- School of Engineering
- School of Education and Humanities (EAH) — *formerly Joel Awoniyi School*
- School of Management Sciences (SMS)
- School of Science and Technology (SAT)
- Veronica Adeleke School of Social Sciences (VASSS)

- School of Law and Security Studies (LSS)
