## Location

Babcock University is located at **Ilishan-Remo, Ogun State, Nigeria**. A satellite campus exists at **Iperu-Remo**, approximately 5 kilometres from the main campus, which houses the School of Law and Security Studies.

Students can leave either on-campus or off-campus.

---

## Main Campus Highlights

The main campus is known for its well-maintained grounds, landscaped environment, and distinct architecture. The university's **BUCLEAN** (Babcock University Campus Landscape and Environmental Amelioration Network) unit manages and maintains the campus ecosystem.

## Key Infrastructure

### Laz Otti Memorial Library
The flagship library facility, formerly known as the Adekunle Alalade Library. Commissioned in **May 2014** and donated by Adventist philanthropist **Dr. Alex Otti**. Key features include:
- Seating for approximately **540 users**
- **42 service providers**
- **E-library** with **135 computer systems** and **30 tablets**
- Access to the **Online Public Access Catalogue (OPAC)**
- Approximately **73,000 volumes** across all disciplines
- Six branch libraries located within schools, departments, and colleges
- Electronic database subscriptions: **EBSCOHOST, AGORA, HINARI, LEXIS NEXIS, HEIN ONLINE, MEDLINE COMPLETE, JSTOR**, and others
- Newspaper, magazine, and journal subscriptions
- Collection of bound periodicals, theses, and dissertations
- **Ellen G. White Research Center** (serves Adventist scholars in 22 countries under the West-Central African Division)
- Uses the **Library of Congress (LC) Classification Scheme** (and Elizabeth Moys for Law materials)
- Open shelf system for browsing

### Babcock University Teaching Hospital (BUTH)
A modern medical facility integral to the College of Health and Medical Sciences. Features include:
- **Cardiac Center** — over 300 open heart surgeries with 100% success rate since 2015
- Radio diagnostic laboratory
- **140-bed facility**
- **600-seater auditorium**
- Medical College complex with research/teaching labs and skill acquisition centre

### Babcock Sports Complex
- Capacity: over **10,000**
- Built in 2009 by the Parents Consultative Forum
- Houses indoor sports pavilion, changing rooms, fitness centre
- Used for graduation ceremonies, evangelical events, and career weeks
- Babcock University is a member of the **All Nigeria Private Universities Games Association (NPUGA)** and happened to host the **2025** edition.

### Central University Cafeteria
- Semi-automated with a dining capacity of **5,000**
- Promotes a **vegetarian menu** in line with Adventist health philosophy
- Offers a balanced, healthy diet

### Babcock Business School Building
- Houses the School of Management Sciences (rebranded from Babcock Business School)
- Smart, air-conditioned classrooms, seminar rooms, and lecture theatres
- Completed June 2012; features a fountain plaza as a landmark attraction

### Student Halls of Residence (Hostels)
- **23 halls of residence** across both the Ilishan and Iperu campuses
- Each hall is equipped with adequate facilities for student comfort

## Commercial and Support Services

### Babcock Investment Group (BIG)
The university's commercial arm includes:
- **Babcock Superstore** — retail provisions and grocery
- **Chinese Restaurant** (near BUSA House)
- Babcock Guest House
- Babcock Block Industry
- Babcock Water Industry
- Babcock Bakery
- Babcock Bookshop

### Babcock Microfinance Bank
A subsidiary of BIG, supporting entrepreneurship development on campus.

### Babcock University Centre for Executive Development
Handles training, executive programmes, conversion courses, seminars, and workshops for organisations and individuals. Also offers Executive Postgraduate Programmes in conjunction with academic departments.

### Hope FM (89.1)
The university's campus radio station, broadcasting from the School of Education and Humanities building. Accessible via internet. Managed by the Division of Management Services and run primarily by students. Programmes include music, news, religious content, and chat shows.

## Spiritual Life Facilities

- **23 worship centers** across campus
- Chaplaincy under the **Division of Spiritual Life (DSL)**
- Staffed by 23 DSL workers and 120 student chaplains
- Weekly services: Morning devotion, Hall worship, Chapel Hour, Mid-week prayer meetings, Friday vespers, Sabbath School, Sabbath worship

## Future Expansion

The University has acquired a **212-acre property** adjacent to the School of Law and Security Studies at Iperu-Remo for future campus expansion.
