## Legal Status and Government Recognition

Babcock University is authorized to operate by virtue of **Decree 9 of 1993** and the **Certificate of Registration as a Private University** issued by the Federal Government of Nigeria in **1999** (Certificate of Registration No. 002). The university is empowered to:

- Provide facilities for learning
- Confer degrees and diplomas
- Conduct research
- Provide whole-person education across approved branches of knowledge

The university does **not** receive government grants.

## Professional Body Accreditations

The following professional bodies have accredited relevant Babcock University programmes:

- **Institute of Chartered Accountants of Nigeria (ICAN)** — Accounting
- **Chartered Institute of Bankers of Nigeria (CIBN)** — Banking and Finance
- **Institute of Chartered Secretaries and Administrators of Nigeria (ICSAN)**
- **Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN)** — Nursing Science
- **Advertising Practitioners' Council of Nigeria (APCON)** — Mass Communication
- **Teachers' Registration Council of Nigeria (TRCN)** — Education programmes
- **Medical and Dental Council of Nigeria (MDCN)** — Medicine (MBBS)
- **Medical Laboratory Science Council of Nigeria (MLSCN)** — Medical Laboratory Science
- **Council of Legal Education (CLE)** — Law (LLB)

## Notable "Firsts" in Accreditation

- Babcock University is the **only Adventist university in Africa** offering programmes in **Medicine and Law**.
- Babcock was one of the **first three private universities** in Nigeria to receive a government charter in 1999.
- The Law School (located at Iperu-Remo campus) has **full accreditation** from both the Nigeria Legal Council and the NUC.
- The Medical School achieved all levels of MBBS accreditation (Parts I, II, and III) with the first set of MBBS graduates inducted in **January 2017**.
