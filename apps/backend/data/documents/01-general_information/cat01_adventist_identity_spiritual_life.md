## Adventist Identity

Babcock University is a **Seventh-day Adventist (SDA) institution**, fully owned and operated by the SDA Church through the West-Central Africa Division and the three Nigerian Union Conferences (Western, Eastern, and Northern). It is the **largest Seventh-day Adventist university in the world** by enrollment.

The Adventist philosophy of education underpins every aspect of university life — from academic programmes to campus regulations. The integration of faith and learning is not supplementary; it is central to the Babcock experience.

While students are **not required to be Seventh-day Adventist Church members**, all students are expected to **respect and support the university's religious programmes**.

## Division of Spiritual Life (DSL)

The DSL oversees the spiritual wellbeing of the university community. It operates through:
- **23 DSL workers** (pastoral and chaplaincy staff)
- **120 student chaplains** assigned to various departments and hostels
- The University Chaplaincy, which provides prayer, visitation, and psychological-spiritual counselling for students, faculty, and staff

## Outreach and Evangelism

Evangelism has been a core focus since the institution's founding. Key outreach programmes include:
- Branch Sabbath Schools established in surrounding villages (Ikenne, Irolu, Ilara, Odogbolu, and others)
- Prison ministry
- **Nigeria Association of Adventist Students (NAAS)** — Babcock University chapter
- Annual Weeks of Prayer and stadium evangelism programmes, often resulting in baptisms
- Student Message Ambassadors — students sent to establish churches in remote areas during summer

The Religious Studies department's 300 junior students operate branch Sabbath Schools outside the campus as part of their in-service training and practicum.

## Sabbath Observance

As an Adventist institution, Babcock University observes the **Seventh-day Sabbath (sunset Friday to sunset Saturday)**. During this period:
- Secular activities (studying secular materials, commerce, entertainment) are restricted
- Students are expected to attend Sabbath services
- Specific campus regulations apply (see Rules & Regulations — Sabbath Observance)

## Food Philosophy

In line with Adventist health principles, the Central University Cafeteria promotes a **vegetarian menu**, offering students a balanced and healthy diet. The university emphasises the importance of healthful living as part of whole-person development.

## The Ellen G. White Research Center

Located within the Laz Otti Memorial Library, this center serves **Adventist scholars and researchers in 22 countries** under the West-Central African Division. It houses:
- Books, articles, letters, and manuscripts of **Ellen G. White** (co-founder of the Adventist Church, considered a prophetic figure by Adventists)
- Microfilm and microfiche of early Adventist periodicals and writings

The Center was founded on **November 24, 1990** on the ASWA campus under the leadership of President Adekunle Alalade. The first director was **James B. Kio**.
