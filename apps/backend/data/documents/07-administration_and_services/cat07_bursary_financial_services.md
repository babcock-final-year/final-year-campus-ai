## Overview

The **Bursary (Financial Administration Division)** manages all student financial matters including fee collection, student accounts, payment processing, and financial clearances. It operates under the Senior Vice President for Financial Administration.

## Fee Payment

### How to Pay

Fees are paid via **e-transact**


- Full fees are payable **at registration** — no partial registration is permitted
- Sponsored students must make acceptable payment arrangements **before** registration

### Viewing Your Balance

- Log in to the **student portal** to view your account balance
- Detailed statements are available from the Bursary office

---

## Student Account (Bursary Account)

Every registered student has a **Bursary Account** which:
- Tracks fee payments and balances
- Records labour credits (for Work-Study students)
- Is referenced for all financial clearances
- Is the basis for any scholarship/sponsorship postings

### Cash Withdrawals
- Cash withdrawals from the student account are only permitted if there is a genuine excess from **labour credits or non-tagged funds**
- Sponsored students require **express written permission** from the sponsor for any withdrawal

---

## Work-Study Programme

Babcock University operates a **Work-Study Programme** for eligible students:
- Students can earn up to **20% of tuition fees** through approved on-campus work
- Assignments are managed through the Bursary and relevant departments
- Work-Study earnings are credited to the student's Bursary account
- Graduating students must clear any outstanding Work-Study obligations on the Final Clearance Form

## Fee Refund Policy

| Scenario | Refund |
|----------|--------|
| Voluntary withdrawal **before end of Week 4** | Prorated refund based on days in session |
| Withdrawal after Week 4 | No tuition refund |
| Illness / medical emergency | May be prorated over full semester (requires medical documentation) |
| Disciplinary dismissal or abscondment | **No refund under any circumstance** |

## Entrepreneurial Skill Acquisition Programme (ESAP)

- All **400–500L students** must pay **₦15,000** by **Bank Draft** at the University Bursary
- This is in addition to regular fees and must be completed before final clearance

---

## Graduation Financial Clearance

Before graduation, students must obtain **Bursary Clearance** confirming:
1. All fees are fully settled
2. No outstanding balance on the student account
3. All Work-Study obligations are cleared

Form: **Bursary Clearance Form** — download at babcock.edu.ng/student-forms
