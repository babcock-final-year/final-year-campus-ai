## Overview

Babcock University enforces a **100% residency policy** — all students are required to live on campus in the university's halls of residence. There are **20 halls of residence** spread across the main Ilishan-Remo campus and the Iperu-Remo satellite campus.

Each hall is equipped with adequate facilities for student comfort and convenience.

## Hall Check-In Rules

- Students must check in through the **Hall Administrator** using the official **Check-In Form**
- Checking in without the knowledge of hall administration: **₦1,000 per day + immediate eviction**
- Students who complete online registration but do not physically check in: **₦1,000 per day fine**

## Hall Check-Out Rules

- Students must obtain a **Check-Out Form** from the Hall Administrator before leaving
- Improper check-out (with key included): **₦2,000 + cost of lock replacement + door replacement if damaged**
- End-of-year/semester checkout requires clearance from: HOD → Dean → Library → Health Centre → Accommodation → Student Development → Registry → Security

## Residence Hall Programmes

Each hall organises its own social activities for residents and guests:

| Event | Rules |
|-------|-------|
| **Open Day** | Residents expected to remain on campus; active participation required; doors not locked during Open Day hours; no secular music; no alcohol |
| **Hall Banquet/Drama** | Must be approved by Campus Life Committee (CLC) through the Hall Administrator |
| Any other social activity | Must have CLC approval through the Hall Administrator |

---

## Hall Life Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Curfew** | No student may be in the community or outside residence halls between **10:00 pm and 5:45 am**; returning after 9:45 pm without permission: 30 demerit points |
| **Rooms** | Students must be in their own room after 10:00 pm. Being in another student's room after 10:00 pm: 20 demerit points |
| **Cooking** | Cooking of any kind in rooms is **strictly prohibited**. Prohibited appliances (electric cookers, hotplates, etc.) must not be used: 60 demerit points |
| **Worship periods** | No student may lock themselves in or remain in halls during worship periods: 30 demerit points |
| **Nudity** | Walking out of room naked, bathing outside the bathroom: 30 demerit points |
| **Furniture** | Hall furniture (mattresses, tables, chairs) must not be moved: 30 demerit points |
| **Noise** | Music, yelling, and boisterous behaviour in halls: 20–40 demerit points |
| **Property** | Students are discouraged from possessing phones worth more than ₦50,000 (current limit — confirm with VPSD Office). Restitution for theft capped at this amount |

## Hall Administrator

Each hall is managed by a **Hall Administrator** (and Assistant Hall Administrator), who:
- Manages hall check-in and check-out procedures
- Grants permission for students to remain in hall during worship (medical)
- Approves social activities in the hall (in consultation with CLC)
- Implements demerit notices and warnings
- Acts on emergency welfare issues

---

## Married Students

- Married students living with their families may receive concession to own/drive vehicles on campus
- Married female students who become pregnant during the school year must seek clearance from the **VP for Student Development** and move to the community
- Married students are responsible for the medical care and expenses of their family members

---

## Community Students (Off-Campus)

Under exceptional circumstances, some students may live off campus ("community students"). They are still required to:
- Attend all public services (Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays, Sabbath morning and evening) at designated worship centres on campus
- Comply with all university rules and regulations
- Seek permission for all off-campus student activities

> The following categories of students may be granted off-campus living:
> - Married students living with families
> - Students with approved special needs (recommended by SSC, approved by VPSD)
> - Postgraduate students
> - Students employed by the university as drivers
> - Students in staff homes whose parents/guardians permit driving
