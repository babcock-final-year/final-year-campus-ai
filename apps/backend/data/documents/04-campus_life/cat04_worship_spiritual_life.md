## Worship Schedule

Attendance at all religious services is **mandatory**. Students must conduct themselves in harmony with the holiness of service.

| Service | Day(s) | Attendance Check Time | Actual Service |
|---------|--------|-----------------------|----------------|
| **Sabbath Morning Service** | Saturday | 8:30 am – 9:15 am | 8:45 am – 11:00am |
| **Evening Hall Assembly** | Tuesday | 6:00 pm – 6:30 pm | 6:30 pm – 7:30 pm |
| **Evening Services** | Wednesday, Friday, Saturday | 5:30 pm – 6:10 pm | 6:00 pm – 7:00 pm |
| **Babcock Interdisciplinary Seminar (BIS) - Chapel Seminar** | Monday/ Wednesday | 9:00 am – 9:15 am | 9:15 am – 10:00 am |

> Off-campus students must attend public services on **Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Fridays, and Sabbaths** at their designated campus worship centres.

**Rules during worship:**
- Students must not remain in halls/rooms during public gatherings
- Sick students may remain only with a written **Hall Administrator's permit**
- Social gatherings, lectures, and sports are **not permitted** during worship or BIS
- Missing a required meeting: **10 demerit points**; visiting another worship centre during worship: **40–60 demerits**

## Week of Spiritual Revival

- Held **once per session** — a full week of special spiritual revival
- Weekend leaves are **not normally granted** during this week (emergencies excepted)
- All students are expected to be corporately present throughout

## Sabbath Observance (Friday Sundown – Saturday Sundown)

### Friday Preparation
- Clean, iron, and mend clothes before sundown Friday
- Remove clothes from lines before **4:00 pm**
- Hall inspection: **4:00 pm – 5:00 pm**
- Classes end on **Fridays after 1:00pm**
- No sports, lectures, or social gatherings on **Fridays after 3:00 pm**

### Permitted on Sabbath
Attending religious services · meditation · visiting the sick/elderly · reading devotional literature · nature walks · wholesome fellowship

### Prohibited on Sabbath (30 demerit points)
Studying secular material · listening to secular radio · watching television · loitering/wandering · any business transaction (including purchasing food) · laundry/sewing/ironing · participating in secular programmes
