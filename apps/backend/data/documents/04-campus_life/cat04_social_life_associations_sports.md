## Social Life Philosophy

Babcock University encourages **healthy social development** as part of wholistic education. Students find opportunities to build friendships in halls of residence, classrooms, the cafeteria, and at school functions. All social activities on campus must align with the university's Christian values and be approved by the **Campus Life Committee (CLC)**.

---

## Wholesome Relationships

- Friendly relationships between male and female students in **open, group activities** are encouraged
- **Prohibited:** Private walks, clandestine meetings, pair loitering/wandering in the dark, or any unwholesome association
- Courtship must not violate university regulations
- **Marriages:** Students are discouraged from marrying while enrolled. Weddings must take place at the **end of a semester**. Applications for approval must be submitted to Administration **at least 3 months** in advance. No pre-wedding parties on campus during session

## Student Associations

### Academic Associations
Every student is expected to belong to the academic association corresponding to their department. Examples:
- **ASLBU** — Accounting Students' League of Babcock University
- **AGSA** — Agriculture Students' Association
- Other departmental associations exist for every programme

### Cultural Associations
Groups of students of common origin promoting their cultural heritage. All activities must be approved by the Campus Life Committee.

### Babcock University Students Association (BUSA)
- The official student self-governance body
- Governed by a constitution, an elected executive council, and senators representing each hall of residence and community students
- Supported by the Director of Student Activities, an Advisor, Co-Advisor, and a 7-member Council of Patrons (including a Patron-General)
- **Secretariat:** Located at the T-Junction leading to the female hostels
- BUSA constitution copies available at the university library and secretariat
- All student activities must be approved by the **Campus Life Committee (CLC)**

#### BUSA Cabinet Orientation
Before newly elected BUSA cabinet and senate members begin work, the VP for Student Development hosts an orientation focused on Babcock's philosophy of **servant-leadership**. Student leaders may not serve as leaders of any other organisation without approval from the VP for Student Development.

## Social Activity Rules

### General
- All campus social programmes end by **8:45 pm**
- Activities held **Sunday – Thursday only**
- All programme arrangements must be submitted for CLC approval **at least 2 weeks** before the event

### Get-Togethers / Parties
- Must take place **on campus only**
- Written request submitted 2 weeks in advance with: venue, time, organiser(s), club/association name, Academic Staff Sponsor signature, and category of guests

### Off-Campus Activities
- Written request submitted to CLC at least **2 weeks in advance**
- Must include: destination, departure/return times, chaperon(s), transport details
- Signed by: sponsor → hall administrator → VP for Student Development
- Copy of approved request must be returned to all signatories before leaving

## Sports

### Philosophy
Sports at Babcock University serve the **total person** and reflect the four principles of Adventist education: spiritual, social, physical, and mental well-being. **Intramural sports** are emphasised over inter-university competition. Moderation characterises all physical activities.

### Programme Offerings
- Training and recreational activities
- Fitness and endurance programmes
- Inter-Faculty Athletic Competition
- Annual Inter-Faculty Games
- Group matches — Football, Basketball, Volleyball
- Racquet Games Championships
- Cross-Country Race / Road Walk
- Coaching clinics
- Sports club formations (Squash, Golf, Volleyball, Basketball, etc.)

### Sports Complex
The **Babcock Sports Complex** (capacity 10,000+) was built by the Parents Consultative Forum in 2009. It is of international standard and includes:
- Indoor sports pavilion
- Changing and rest rooms
- Administrative offices
- Fitness centre with full equipment

The complex is also used for graduation ceremonies, Week of Spiritual Emphasis, and Career Week.

### Sports Dress Code
- Shorts for sports must be worn **only at the sports arena**
- Female students' sports attire must still comply with overall dress regulations

## Student Personal Account (Bursary)

A deposit system where students (or sponsors) pre-load funds at the Bursary for incidentals. Cash withdrawals from the regular student account are only permitted if there is an excess from labour credits or non-tagged sources. Sponsored students require express sponsor permission for any withdrawals.
