## Automobile / Vehicle Policy

University students are **not permitted** to own or drive vehicles on campus or in neighbouring communities (Ilishan, Ilara, Iperu, Ikenne, etc.).

### Exceptions (Concession Categories)

The following categories of students may apply for vehicle registration and permission:

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Married students living with their families | — |
| Students employed by the university as drivers | — |
| Students in staff homes whose parents/guardians permit them to drive | — |
| Postgraduate students | — |
| Students with approved special needs | Strictly recommended by SSC and approved by VP Student Development |

### Conditions for Approved Students
1. Must have a **Citizenship Grade B or above** in the previous semester
2. Apply in writing to the **VP for Student Development**
3. If approved, register with the **Director of Security Services**

### Violations
- First offence: **Fine**
- Repeated violations: **Withdrawal of registration**
- Owning/keeping/driving a vehicle on campus without permission: **60 demerit points** (Third-degree misconduct)

## Security Services

The Campus Security Department provides trained uniformed security personnel to maintain safety across campus.

### Services Provided
- Protection of lives and property of students and staff on and off campus
- Transportation support for BUTH (transporting sick staff/students to and from hospital)
- Sounding of fire alarms
- Enforcing Babcock University standards (Student and Staff handbooks)
- Receiving, registering, and returning lost-and-found items to identified owners
- Mounting surveillance on and off campus to identify threats and trespassers
- Supporting examinations to ensure compliance with Babcock standards
- Escort services for dignitaries
- Liaising with state security for Community Policing
- Enforcing traffic and pedestrian rules on campus

### Emergency Contacts
- **Security Emergency 1:** 08149514646
- **Security Emergency 2:** 09055908963

### Babcock Traffic and Safety Management Authority
Enforces all existing university rules for free flow of vehicular and pedestrian movement on campus; provides directional services to visitors.

## Fire Safety

The **Fire Safety Department** (under Management Services) ensures:
- All staff receive fire safety training at periodic intervals
- Fire prevention measures are in place across campus
- In the event of a fire outbreak, it is promptly, efficiently, and safely contained

---

## Personal Property & Phones

- Students are **discouraged** from bringing phones worth more than **₦50,000** (current university-set limit — confirm with VPSD Office, subject to periodic review)
- In the event of loss or theft, the amount recoverable (where restitution applies) shall not exceed this limit
- Selling electronic items (laptops, phones, cameras, tablets) to/from other students without written **Security Service approval**: **60 demerit points**
- All found/lost items are handled by the Security Department

## Campus Environment

### BUCLEAN
The **Babcock University Campus Landscape and Environmental Amelioration Network (BUCLEAN)** manages and maintains the university's ecosystem, keeping the campus clean, welcoming, and conducive for learning. Littering and lawn-crossing: **20 demerit points** (plus mandatory cleaning of the immediate area).

### ICT Department
The **ICT Department** is the hub of the university's information technology infrastructure. It provides:
- Central IT Service Desk for staff and student support
- Seamless interconnectivity between the university and the outside world
- Cybersecurity and IT service management
- Support for teaching, research, and administrative IT systems

## Student ID Card

- Every student must carry a valid Babcock University **ID card** at all times on campus
- Required for library access, hall entry, examinations, and other university functions
- **Failure to wear ID card:** 30 demerit points
- ID cards are submitted at Security upon final check-out / withdrawal
