**Category:** Campus Life

## Overview

Every Babcock University student is assessed on **citizenship** alongside academics. A Citizenship Grade is awarded each semester based on merit points earned and demerit points incurred. **No student may graduate with a cumulative citizenship grade below C.**

> Merit points are reset at the end of every semester. However, the **consequences of demerit points may carry over** beyond the semester and academic session.

---

## Citizenship Grading Scale

| Grade | Merit Points Range |
|-------|--------------------|
| **A** (Student A) | 50 – 60 points |
| **B** (Student B) | 40 – 49 points |
| **C** (Student C) | 30 – 39 points |
| **D** (Student D) | 20 – 29 points |
| **E** (Student E) | 10 – 19 points |
| **F** (Student F) | Below 10 points |

> Minimum for graduation: **Grade C** (cumulative)

---

## Demerit Point Thresholds & Consequences

| Demerit Accumulated | Consequence |
|---------------------|-------------|
| Up to 20 points | Official caution by Hall Administrator; counselling sessions begin |
| Up to 30 points | Warning letter from Hall Administrator; parents informed |
| Up to 40 points | Biological parents + student invited before **Deans Council (DC)** for second and final caution |
| Above 40 points | DC submits recommendations to **Student Disciplinary Committee (SDC)** → **Administrative Committee (ADCOM)** |

## Financial Fines

| Offence | Fine |
|---------|------|
| Damage to university property | Double the total replacement cost + labour |
| Graffiti | Double cost of repainting + 20 demerit points |
| Lost property | Replacement cost |
| Serious vandalism (permanent damage) | Replacement + 30 demerit points |
| Individual's lost/stolen property (phones) | Max recoverable: ₦50,000 (university-set limit, subject to periodic review — confirm with VPSD Office) |
| Improper check-in | ₦1,000 per day + immediate eviction from hall |
| Improper check-out (with key) | ₦2,000 + lock replacement + door replacement if damaged |
| Injuries inflicted on student/faculty/staff | Double total treatment and transportation cost + additional sanctions |
| Off-campus students using hall facilities | ₦1,000/day (regular hall), ₦2,000/day (premium/classic halls) |
| Failure to complete hall registration (check-in) after online registration | ₦1,000 per day |

## Disciplinary Bodies

| Body | Role |
|------|------|
| **Deans Council (DC)** | Handles 1st and 2nd degree misconduct; summons parents at 40 demerit accumulation |
| **ADCOM (Administrative Committee)** | Receives DC/SDC recommendations; may appoint Ad-Hoc Committee for urgent cases |
| **Adhoc Committee** | Handles special/urgent cases as delegated by ADCOM |
| **Students Disciplinary Committee (SDC)** | Processes serious cases referred by DC for ADCOM consideration |
| **Students Appeal Committee (SAC)** | Handles student appeals against disciplinary decisions |

### Appeals
- Legal proceedings may only be instituted **after 14 days** from receipt of the decision in writing
- A student who unsuccessfully pursues legal action may not be re-admitted
- All appeals for new-semester consideration must be submitted within **15 days of resumption**
- Appeals directed to: **Secretary, Students Appeal Committee, Babcock University** (on prescribed Appeal Form from Registry)
