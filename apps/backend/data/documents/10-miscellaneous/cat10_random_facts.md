## 🏛️ History & Identity

- **BU is one of Nigeria's first three private universities**, chartered on May 10, 1999 — alongside Igbinedion University and Madonna University. Before that, all Nigerian universities were government-owned.

- **The university is older than it looks.** Despite being chartered in 1999, the institution has existed since **1959** — first as the Adventist College of West Africa (ACWA), then as the Adventist Seminary of West Africa (ASWA) from 1975. That's over 65 years of history.

- **The first graduating class had only 7 students.** In 1962, the very first graduates of ACWA received a Diploma in Theology — a far cry from the 3,500+ students who graduate today.

- **The university is named after a man who never visited Nigeria.** David Comfort Babcock (1854–1932) was an American Seventh-day Adventist missionary administrator and benefactor who supported the establishment of the institution from abroad.

- **Babcock University is the largest Seventh-day Adventist university in the world**, with over 13,000 enrolled students — bigger than Andrews University (the SDA flagship in Michigan, USA).

- **BU is credited with kickstarting the private university revolution in Nigeria.** When it was chartered in 1999, fewer than 5 private universities existed in Nigeria. By 2018, that number had grown to over 60 — a movement BU helped catalyse.

## 🌍 Scale & Numbers

- **Over 21,000 alumni** have passed through the institution across its three eras (ACWA, ASWA, and BU) — spread across Nigeria and multiple countries.

- **The 23rd Convocation (2025) produced 243 First Class graduates** out of 2,543 undergraduates — nearly 1 in 10 graduates achieved First Class Honours.

- **The best graduating student in 2025, Lawal Oyiza, scored a CGPA of 4.97 out of 5.00** — missing a perfect score by just 0.03 points.

- **BU has over 33 worship centres** spread across its campus — more than most entire towns have churches.

- **The campus has 20+ halls of residence** housing thousands of students under a strict 100% residency policy. Every single student must live on campus — no exceptions.

- **A single graduating class (24th Matriculation, 2023) admitted 3,727 new students** — roughly the entire population of a small Nigerian town, joining in one ceremony.

## 🏥 Medicine & Health

- **BUTH's cardiac centre has a 100% open-heart surgery success rate since 2015**, having performed over 300 such surgeries. That is an extraordinary record for any hospital, public or private, in West Africa.

- **The Ben Carson School of Medicine is named after the famous neurosurgeon** Dr. Ben Carson — the same man who separated conjoined twins, pioneered hemispherectomy surgery, and later served as U.S. Secretary of Housing and Urban Development. BU's medical school carries his name.

- **BU's teaching hospital (BUTH) is open to the general public**, not just students and staff — making it one of the few private university hospitals in Nigeria that serves the broader community.

## 🎓 Academics

- **Students who miss the 70% attendance threshold for any course cannot sit the final exam** — no matter how prepared they are. Attendance literally determines exam eligibility.

- **Bringing a phone into the exam hall — even if it's switched off — earns a 1-year suspension.** The phone doesn't have to be in use. Mere possession is enough.

- **BU offers French as a degree programme**, making it one of the few Nigerian universities where you can graduate with a B.A. in French — complete with courses on 16th-century French literature and advanced oral expression.

- **Estate Management students study French** as part of their professional practice courses (BU-ESM 208 and BU-ESM 308 are literally titled "Professional Practices French"). Apparently, international real estate dealings demand it.

- **The Research Project (final year thesis) is worth 6 credit units** across most programmes — the single highest-credit course in any student's academic career.

- **BU uses a 5-point GPA scale**, not the 4-point scale used by most American universities. A "First Class" requires 4.50–5.00 — the equivalent of a 3.6+ in American terms.

## 🎵 Famous Alumni

- **Davido enrolled at Babcock University in 2011** to study Music — and reportedly co-wrote his breakout hit "Back When" while still a student. He graduated in 2015 as one of BU's most famous alumni.

- **BNXN (Buju), one of Nigeria's biggest Afrobeats stars, studied Theatre Arts at BU** — which perhaps explains why his stage presence and storytelling are so compelling.

- **Priscilla Ojo, actress and daughter of Iyabo Ojo, enrolled at BU in 2017** for Theatre Arts and graduated in 2021 — following a growing tradition of Nollywood talent coming through the school.

- **Debo Ogundoyin, who studied Agronomy at BU, became the youngest Speaker of the Oyo State House of Assembly** at the time of his election in 2019 — a BU graduate running one of Nigeria's most populous state legislatures.

- **BU alumnus Olamide Samuel became the youngest African to earn a PhD in National Security Studies**, achieving it at age 25 after graduating from BU's International Law & Diplomacy programme in 2013.

## 📻 Media & Broadcasting

- **BU operates its own licensed FM radio station — Hope FM 89.1 MHz**, broadcasting across Ogun and Lagos States since May 31, 2012. Most universities in Nigeria don't have one.

- **The campus also houses the AWR (Adventist World Radio) Nigeria Studio**, which produces approximately **1,456 radio programmes per year** for international broadcast. That's nearly 4 programmes every single day.

- **A full WAD Media Centre was established on campus in 2019** for the West-Central Africa Division — making BU not just a university, but a regional media production hub.

## 🏗️ Infrastructure

- **The BBS Building (opened June 2012) cost ₦900 million to construct** — at a time when that was an extraordinary sum for a private Nigerian institution to invest in a single building.

- **The Sports Complex, built in 2009, can seat over 10,000 people** — larger than many professional stadiums in Nigeria. It was funded by the PCF.

- **The Laz Otti Memorial Library holds approximately 63,000 volumes** and provides access to over 5,000 electronic journals — a collection larger than many state public libraries.

- **BU has its own microfinance bank on campus** — the Babcock Microfinance Bank — offering financial services to students and the local community.

- **The campus has its own primary and secondary schools** (Babcock Staff School and High School), effectively making it a self-contained educational community from nursery to postgraduate level.

## ⛪ Adventist Identity

- **BU observes the Sabbath every week**, meaning academic activities, entertainment, and social events are all restricted from Friday sunset to Saturday sunset. This isn't symbolic — it's enforced, with 30 demerit points for violations.

- **Every student is assigned to a specific worship centre** from 33 on-campus options and is expected to attend Saturday worship services. Attendance is tracked.

- **The Ellen G. White (EGW) Research Centre on campus was established in 1990** — making it one of the earliest repositories of Adventist historical writings in West Africa. EGW (1827–1915) is a foundational figure in SDA theology.

- **BU is part of the second-largest Christian school system in the world** — the global SDA education network, which operates thousands of schools across every continent.

## 🎓 Campus Life Quirks

- **Jeans are banned in every form, always** — including jeggings, jean skirts, and denim shorts. Not just in class. Not just on Sundays. Always. On campus. At any BU event. Everywhere.

- **Students start each semester with 60 merit points and can only lose them** — there's no way to earn additional points beyond your starting balance. Every violation chips away at what you have.

- **Cooking in your hostel room attracts 60 demerit points** — the highest single-offence penalty short of suspension-level misconduct. One illicit hotplate can cost you your entire semester's conduct standing.

- **The phone value limit for students is ₦50,000** — BU places a cap on how expensive a phone a student can own on campus, presumably to reduce theft and social inequality.

- **Friday afternoon (3–5 PM) is mandatory room inspection time every single week** — sports, clubs, and all activities must pause so students can clean their rooms before Sabbath. Missing it is a demerit offence.

- **The curfew is 10:00 PM**, but the demerit clock starts at **9:45 PM** — giving students exactly 15 minutes of grace between "heading back" and "you're late."

- **Opposite-sex visitation is banned on Fridays and Saturdays entirely** — the only days with zero visitation hours are the two days of Sabbath preparation and rest.
