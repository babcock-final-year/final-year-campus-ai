## Overview

Babcock University has a **zero tolerance policy** for any form of academic dishonesty. Morally and spiritually, the institution is committed to scholastic integrity. Both students and staff are required to maintain high ethical, Christian levels of honesty in all spheres of academic life.

---

## What Counts as Academic Dishonesty

- **Plagiarism** — presenting another's words, information, or ideas as your own
- **Unauthorized use of notes or textbooks** (hard or digital) during quizzes or examinations
- **Copying or spying** on another student's test paper (in formal or take-home exams)
- **Talking to another student** during an examination
- **Unauthorized collaboration** on assignments marked as individual work
- Any form of impersonation or submission of work not one's own

---

## Consequences

Academic dishonesty automatically results in:
- A **failing grade** for the affected examination or assessment
- **Suspension** from the University, or
- **Outright dismissal** from the University

All academic dishonesty cases are referred to the **Senate Panel on Examination and Academic Misconduct (SPEAM)**, which investigates and makes recommendations to the Senate. Penalties are further detailed in the Student Handbook.

---

## Responsibilities

### Teacher Responsibilities
- Clearly explain all requirements and regulations for assignments, examinations, and projects (e.g., whether open book or peer collaboration is permitted)
- State collaboration rules explicitly in the course outline (syllabus)
- "No collaboration" is the default assumption unless the teacher states otherwise

### Student Responsibilities
- Take responsibility for avoiding plagiarism by learning proper citation and attribution procedures
- Consult lecturers when unsure how to cite sources
- Assume all coursework is "no collaboration" unless explicitly stated otherwise
- Endeavour to have at least one textbook per course

### Departmental Responsibility
- Some departments have additional honesty policies specific to their programme that carry the same force as university-wide policy
- Students will be informed of such policies before implementation

---

## Procedure When Dishonesty Is Suspected

1. The lecturer or invigilator must **confront the student** with the suspected dishonesty and **document the confrontation** before witnesses (if available)
2. The case is referred to **SPEAM**
3. SPEAM notifies the department and investigates
4. A student may **appeal** Senate's decision — either for leniency or on grounds of misjudgment
5. **Legal representation is not allowed** before university committees

---

## Academic Integrity and Research

All graduating students are required to complete a **research project** prior to graduation. Research must adhere to ethical standards and proper attribution of sources. Students must ensure their research projects are original and properly documented.
