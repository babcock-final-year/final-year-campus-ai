# Babcock University — Academic Policies

**Category:** Academics
**Subcategory:** Academic Policies
**Source:** Undergraduate Bulletin 2019–2023
**Last Updated:** 7th March 2026
**Format:** Markdown

---

## Academic Session Structure

- The academic year runs from the **first Monday of September** to the **last weekend of April** the following year.
- The year is divided into **two semesters** (First and Second).
- A student is expected to spend a **minimum of 3 hours per week** for every credit unit registered.

---

## Registration

- Registration dates are published each semester in the academic calendar.
- Registration is complete only after all required procedures are satisfied, including a medical examination for new students.
- **Late registration** attracts a late fee, and the course load is reduced by one credit for each week of instruction missed.
- No student may register after the published last day to enter any class.

### Adding and Dropping Courses
- Students may add/drop courses within a semester up to the officially approved date for the Mid-Semester examination.
- The exact deadline is published in the academic calendar each semester.

## Change of Programme

- A student must have completed **at least two semesters** in their current programme before applying for a change.
- Application is made through the Department, then Registry and Student Support Services.
- It is advisable to initiate a change request at the **end of the second semester**.
- Students changing programme may have course credit disparities that need resolution.

---

## Sequence of Courses / Prerequisites

- A student may not receive credit for a course that is a **prerequisite** to a course for which the student already received credit.
- Students are not allowed to take upper-level courses without completing lower-level prerequisites.

---

## Student Residency and Duration

- Normal residency is aligned with NUC stipulations for each programme.
- A maximum of **2 extra years** are allowed beyond the normal programme duration.
- A student who discontinues for **one academic year or more** must apply for resumption.
- Discontinuation without permission leads to **loss of studentship**.

---

## Auditing Classes

- Students may attend classes without earning grades (auditing) only with **official Registrar permission**.
- Auditing requires attendance of at least **80% of classes and labs**.
- Tuition charges for auditing are the same as for regular enrolment.
- Grade awarded: **AU** (no credit).
- Students who later want credit must retake the class.

---

## SIWES — Student Industrial Work Experience Scheme

- SIWES is a **6-month industrial attachment** required for eligible programmes, run January–June.
- Only programmes accredited for SIWES by ITF and NUC are eligible.
- Students returning from SIWES complete their Second Semester in a **Post-SIWES Second Semester** (first week of July to last week of August), then resume normal First Semester in September.

---

## Academic Trips

### Eligibility
- Students on academic probation are **disqualified** from academic trips.

### Approval Process
1. **Intent letter** addressed to the SVP through Head of Department and Dean — must include evidence that the trip is in the course outline, invitation letter (if applicable), cost per student, and fund generation method.
2. On SVP approval, apply through the Associate VP for Institutional Effectiveness using the official form.
3. All off-campus activities require submission at least **two weeks** in advance.

### Student Requirements on a Trip
- Complete a pre-trip assignment for submission on return.
- Make a class presentation on return.
- Students who opt out must complete an alternative make-up assignment.

---

## Withdrawal Policies

| Type | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Academic Withdrawal** | CGPA below 2.00 triggers probation; persistent below 2.00 leads to suspension |
| **Voluntary Withdrawal** | Complete official withdrawal forms from Registry; records and grades retained; may reapply within 2 years |
| **Disciplinary Withdrawal** | Readmission requires Senate approval with conditions |
| **Health Withdrawal** | Requires certification by university-approved Medical Officer; readmission conditional on clean bill of health |

---

## Tuition Refund Policy

- Refunds are only available for withdrawals completed **before the end of the fourth week** of the semester.
- Charges are prorated based on actual days school was in session.
- Board/room refunds are prorated based on actual days until the student checks out.
- For illness, accident, or emergency, charges may be prorated over the entire semester.
- **No refunds** for withdrawals due to disciplinary action or abscondment.

---

## Textbook Policy

- Students are advised to budget **₦30,000–₦40,000 per semester** for textbooks, depending on discipline.
- Textbook costs are **not** included in university fee estimates.
- Lecturers are prohibited from coercing students to buy unapproved textbooks for assignments or tests. Violations are referred to the Staff Disciplinary Committee.

---

## Students with Disabilities

Students with disabilities are required to:
1. Report to **Student Support Services** for assessment at the start of the semester (or as soon as the disability occurs)
2. Present clearance/recommendations to relevant university officials
3. Maintain ongoing contact with Student Support Services

---

## General Graduation Requirements

1. Complete the minimum required semester credits as published per department
2. Earn minimum passing grade (Core = 50%; GEDS = 40%) in all credits
3. Achieve a minimum **CGPA of 2.50** on the 5-point scale
4. Complete an approved **research project** prior to graduation
5. A second baccalaureate degree requires at least half of additional credits beyond the first degree
6. Students graduating with Third Class must have a CGPA of 2.00–2.49 while having passed all requirements
