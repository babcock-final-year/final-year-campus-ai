## 5-Point Grading Scale

Babcock University uses a **5-point grading system** adopted by the University Senate:

| Grade | Marks Range | Quality Points | Definition |
|-------|------------|----------------|------------|
| A | 80 – 100 | 5.00 | Superior |
| B | 60 – 79 | 4.00 | Above Average |
| C | 50 – 59 | 3.00 | Average |
| D | 45 – 49 | 2.00 | Below Average |
| E | 40 – 44 | 1.00 | Pass |
| F | 0 – 39 | 0.00 | Fail |

## Non-Point Grades

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **I** | Incomplete — due to illness or recognized emergency. Must be resolved by end of the following semester, or automatically becomes F. Does not affect GPA. |
| **S** | Satisfactory (no grade point value) |
| **U** | Unsatisfactory (no grade point value) |
| **W** | Withdrawal (no grade point value) |
| **WV** | Waiver (no grade point value) |
| **AU** | Audited — no academic credit awarded |
| **FA** | Failure due to unauthorized absence |

## CGPA Calculation Formula

```
CGPA = Σ(CR × QP) / Σ(CR)

Where:
  CR = Credit value for each course (passed or failed)
  QP = Quality Points earned from grade for each course
```

All courses taken — including repeated courses — are counted in the CGPA calculation.

## Degree Classification

Babcock University **does not issue Ordinary Pass degrees**. Degree classification is as follows:

| Class | CGPA Range |
|-------|-----------|
| First Class | 4.50 – 5.00 |
| Second Class Upper | 3.50 – 4.49 |
| Second Class Lower | 2.50 – 3.49 |
| Third Class | 2.00 – 2.49 |

> A minimum CGPA of **2.50** is required to graduate. A student who has passed all requirements but has a CGPA between 2.00–2.49 may graduate with Third Class.

## Continuous Assessment Breakdown

By Senate policy, **Continuous Assessment (CA) = 40%** of the final course grade, with the Final Exam = 60%.

| Component | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Class Attendance | 5% |
| Quizzes & Tests | 10% |
| Assignments | 10% |
| Mid-Semester Exam | 15% |
| **Total CA** | **40%** |
| Final Semester Exam | 60% |
| **Grand Total** | **100%** |

### Additional CA Rules
- Students can write a maximum of **4 short periodic tests** per semester (not scheduled in the last 3 weeks of classes).
- Essay/research assignment deadline: **8 calendar days** before end of classes.
- All test papers must be returned to students **before** the final examination.
- Class participation marks may not exceed **10%**.
- Workload limits for written assignments (excluding final exam):
  - 100-level: 2,500 words
  - 200-level: 3,000 words
  - 300-level: 3,500 words
  - 400/500-level: 4,000 words

## Repeating a Course

- A student may repeat a failed course **only once** without the repeat counting against First Class eligibility.
- All subsequent repeats count fully in CGPA computation.
- The original **Fail grade is not expunged** from the record.
- When repeating a course with practicals, the practicals must also be repeated.
- A student who earns F or WP in a sequence course must repeat it before proceeding to the next in the sequence.

### First Class Ineligibility Conditions
A student is **not eligible for First Class** if they:
1. Have repeated any course
2. Have not carried a minimum of 16 credit units each semester (except the last semester)
3. Have not completed the last half of their degree requirements in residence at Babcock

## Academic Probation

| Status | Trigger |
|--------|---------|
| Academic Probation | CGPA falls below 2.00 at end of any semester |
| Suspension | CGPA remains below 2.00 for two consecutive semesters |
| Withdrawal | GPA of 1.00 or less in any semester; or recurring suspension |

- A student on probation registers for **minimum 12, maximum 16 credits**.
- A 100-level student with CGPA below 2.00 after two consecutive semesters may repeat or change programme without suspension.
- A suspended student may reapply after **one full academic year**.
- A student dismissed after readmission **may not be re-admitted again**.

## Grievance and Re-grading

### Grievance Process
1. Approach the lecturer directly (within 5 working days)
2. Escalate to Head of Department (within 2 working days)
3. Escalate to School Dean (within 2 working days)
4. Submit written request for Grievance Committee review

### Regrading Fee
- Application fee: **₦5,000.00** (subject to review)
- If student wins: grade updated, lecturer reprimanded, fee refunded
- If student loses: letter of reprimand, original grade retained

## Transcripts

- Issued by the **Registrar's Office** on written, signed student request
- First copy (**Student Copy**) is **free**
- Subsequent copies attract a fee and are sent directly to the requesting institution
- No transcript released if outstanding financial obligations exist
- Transcripts can be issued even for expelled students, per BU's redemptive philosophy
