## Philosophy

Assessment at Babcock University is designed to:
- Develop and demonstrate a range of intellectual abilities
- Measure student achievement against institutional and international standards
- Monitor progress and diagnose learning strengths and weaknesses
- Address individual differences through a variety of formative and summative tasks
- Inform curriculum design and teaching improvements

Assessment must be characterised by:
- **Content Validity** — measures what it claims to measure
- **Predictive Validity** — predicts success in desired career or ministry outcomes
- **Suitability** — appropriate to the discipline
- **Reliability** — consistent and fair evaluation

---

## Modes of Assessment

Students may be assessed through coursework, subject examinations, or a combination of both. All written and oral assessments use **English medium** (except non-English language exams).

Assessment modes include:
- Written examinations (short answer, multiple choice, open book)
- Essays, reading reports, research assignments
- Independent research projects and field work
- Class presentations and oral assessments
- Portfolios, artifacts, works of art
- Process diaries, workbooks, project work, laboratory reports

Assessment rigor increases with academic level:
- 100-level: may use multiple choice and short answer
- 200-level: less reliance on multiple choice
- 300-level and above: primarily extended response, analysis, synthesis, and argumentation

---

## Moderation and External Examiners

- **External moderators** (currently teaching at a recognized university) are appointed for each discipline area
- They serve for **2-year terms** and review examination scripts and assignments
- Their role is to ensure fair evaluation and maintain rigorous academic standards
- All results pass through:
  1. **Departmental Committee of Examiners (DCE)** — meets within 2 weeks of exams; moderates borderline results
  2. **School Board of Examiners (SBE)** — reviews and accepts DCE recommendations before Senate approval

---

## Student Rating of Teaching

- The **Student Rating of Teaching Effectiveness Instrument** is administered in at least one subject per teacher per semester
- Conducted by the Head of Department between **Weeks 10–12**
- Results submitted to the Associate VP for Institutional Effectiveness
- Each teacher is formally evaluated at least **once every 3 years**

---

## Course Outlines (Syllabi)

Every lecturer is required to prepare a **detailed course outline (syllabus)** that includes:
- Course description
- Detailed assessment breakdown (percentage contribution of each component)
- Schedule of assessments (to be communicated to students at the start of each semester)
- Collaboration rules and special requirements

A copy is kept in the department. Students receive their copy at the start of every course.

---

## Missed Examinations

- A student who misses an examination due to illness or genuine emergency must report in writing to the Registrar (through Dean, HOD, and Lecturer), attaching certified evidence.
- If approved, the student is allowed to write the exam at a rescheduled time.
- If a student writes the exam despite illness, the recorded grade stands.

---

## Examination Day Rules

- No additional assessment (quizzes, make-up tests) may be conducted on the day of the final examination.
- This protects students from undue pressure during their final exam.
