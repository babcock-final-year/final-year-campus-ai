## Overview

Babcock University welcomes students from across Africa and the world. As a pan-African Seventh-day Adventist institution, BU has always enrolled students from other West African nations and beyond. International students benefit from dedicated support services designed to help them adjust academically and socially to life in Nigeria.

---

## Admission Requirements for International Students

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Academic credentials | Must be certified equivalents of Nigerian O'Level and UTME requirements (or Direct Entry equivalents) |
| West African students | May use WAEC/NECO results; some countries' results accepted directly |
| Non-West African students | Must provide certified transcripts and equivalency letter from NUC or relevant body |
| English proficiency | Must demonstrate proficiency in English (language of instruction) |
| Visa / immigration | Student visa required for non-ECOWAS citizens; obtain from Nigerian Embassy/High Commission |
| Medical records | Must provide immunisation records; health screening on arrival |
| Application portal | https://admissions.babcock.edu.ng |

## Support Services for International Students

All international student support is coordinated through the **Student Support Centre (SSC)**:

| Service | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **SSC International Care** | Contact: Joshua Umahi (SSC) |
| **Rules & regulations orientation** | Specialised briefing on BU's unique conduct code |
| **Socio-cultural acclimatisation** | Support in navigating Nigerian culture and campus life |
| **Visa documentation assistance** | Help with documentation for immigration compliance |
| **Welfare monitoring** | Regular welfare check-ins for students adjusting to campus life |
| **Parent connectivity** | SSC liaises with parents/guardians abroad |

## Campus Life for International Students

- A campus that is home to ~13,000 students from across Nigeria and West Africa
- Students represent diverse ethnic, national, and cultural backgrounds 
- The **SDA community structure** of BU means a familiar environment for Adventist students from SDA schools abroad
- **Hope FM 89.1** broadcasts in English and can help students stay connected with current affairs
