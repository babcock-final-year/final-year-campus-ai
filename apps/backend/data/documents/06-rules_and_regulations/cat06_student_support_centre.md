## Overview

The **Student Support Centre (SSC)** is the primary care centre for Babcock University students and their families. It comprises the **Counselling Unit** and the **Social Work Unit**, staffed by qualified counsellors and social workers.

**Location:** Near Gideon Troopers Hall

All SSC services are provided in a **confidential and caring environment**.

---

## Services Offered

### Counselling & Development
- Career planning and guidance
- Study habit skills training
- Time management coaching
- Relationship counselling
- Behaviour modification
- Academic counselling
- Psychosocial counselling
- Life crises and adjustment support
- Substance abuse intervention, counselling, and rehabilitation
- Bereavement support services
- Psychological testing services
- Advocacy and linkages for medical challenges

### Social Work Unit
- Welfare assessments
- Advocacy for students with special circumstances
- Social integration support

### Special Duties
- First Year Experience Programme (orientation support for 100L students)
- International student support and acclimatisation

---

## International Students

SSC provides specialised support for international students transitioning into the Nigerian campus environment at Babcock University, including:
- Rules and regulations orientation
- Socio-cultural acclimatisation assistance
- Ongoing welfare monitoring

---

## Parent Support

Parents and families of enrolled students can access SSC for:
- Parent conferences and orientation
- Referrals and consultancy on parenting or academic planning issues

---

## Students With Special Needs

Babcock University works to provide a conducive environment for students with special needs. Students must:

1. Report to the SSC for **assessment** and obtain a **clearance/recommendation letter**
2. Present the clearance to relevant university officials at the start of each semester (or when the need arises)
3. Maintain **ongoing contact** with the SSC throughout their studies

---

## Psychosocial Screening

All students are periodically screened to ascertain their psychosocial fitness. Refusal to submit to screening or absconding from it is a **Tier 2 suspension offence** (see `cat06_suspension_dismissal_appeals.md`).

## SSC & Readmission

Students returning from suspension must begin their readmission process through the SSC and complete a **3-month Special Rehabilitation Programme** during their first semester back (at own expense).
