## Overview

Academic integrity is a core value at Babcock University. All cases of academic misconduct are handled by the **Senate Panel on Examination and Academic Misconduct (SPEAM)**. Penalties are severe and non-negotiable.

> Any student involved in examination misconduct who has been **previously disciplined** for any disciplinary issue faces **expulsion** on the second offence.

---

## Senate Penalties for Examination Misconduct

| # | Offence | Penalty |
|---|---------|---------|
| i | Involvement in examination leakage, or gaining prior knowledge (whether true or untrue) of examination questions and **failing to report it** within 15 hours | Minimum **2 years suspension** |
| ii | Writing before or after official timing | Paper **cancelled** + **1 year suspension** |
| iii(a) | Copying, aiding others to copy, or cooperating with another candidate's request for help in the exam hall | Failing grade in the course + minimum **1 year suspension** (student must physically leave campus) |
| iii(b) | Exchanging answer papers in the examination hall | Same as iii(a) |
| iv | Copying from or referring to textbooks, notebooks, phones, chips, or other unauthorised materials | Same as iii(a) |
| v | Bringing unauthorised aids into exam (phones, calculators, organisers, bags, etc.) | Failing grade in the course + minimum **1 year suspension** |
| vi(a) | Smuggling blank or completed answer scripts **into** the exam hall | Failing grade in the course + **2 years suspension** |
| vi(b) | Smuggling completed answer scripts **out of** the exam hall | Failing grade in the course + minimum **2 years suspension** |
| vii | Chatting, disturbance, verbal/non-verbal communication, changing seat without permission, noisemaking, or standing up in the exam hall | Failing grade in the course + **1 year suspension** |
| viii | Writing examination **for another student** (impersonation) | **Expulsion** and **prosecution** of both parties; a non-student impersonator is referred to Security |
| ix | Possession of dangerous weapons in examination | **Expulsion** and **prosecution** |
| x | Assaulting or fighting an invigilator or university officer assigned for examinations | Verbal assault: minimum **1 year suspension** · Physical assault: **expulsion** |
| xi | Refusing to surrender incriminating evidence, chewing/destroying it | Failing grade in the course + **2 years suspension** |
| xii(a) | Insubordination — refusing to sign the incidence form or write a statement as demanded | Minimum **1 year suspension** |
| xii(b) | Disobedience to the instruction of an invigilator or university staff in an exam situation | Failing grade in the course + minimum **1 year suspension** |
| xiii | Refusing to give evidence on request by an invigilator/investigation panel | Witness: **1 year suspension** · Culprit: **2 years suspension** |
| xiv | Examination misconduct by a student previously disciplined for examination misconduct | **Expulsion** |
| xv | Forging any document relevant to the examination (e.g. Financial Clearance Form) | **Expulsion** |
| xvi | Impersonation using another student's course form, exam clearance, or ID card | Failing grade in the course + **1 year suspension** |
| xvii | Disciplinary offence by a student who has been **previously suspended once** for any disciplinary issue | **Expulsion** |
| xviii | Disciplinary offence by a student who has received **one prior warning** for any disciplinary issue | Minimum **1 year suspension** |
| xix | Staff involvement in exam leakage, aiding/abetting/condoning examination misconduct | **Dismissal** and **prosecution** |

---

## General Academic Integrity Rules

- Students must not aid, abet, or ignore knowledge of examination misconduct
- All suspected leakages must be reported to university authorities **within 15 hours** of gaining knowledge
- Suspension sanctions begin from the **semester of the infraction**
- Suspended students must **physically leave campus** for the full duration
- All cases are processed through SPEAM → ADCOM → possible appeal to VC via SAC

---

## Continuous Assessment (CA) Integrity

- Attendance is mandatory and forms **5% of CA**
- Quizzes and assignments are individual work unless explicitly stated otherwise
- Submission of another student's assignment or plagiarised work constitutes academic dishonesty
- Mid-semester examinations are subject to the same conduct rules as final exams

---

## SPEAM Composition & Process

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **SPEAM** | Senate Panel on Examination and Academic Misconduct — investigates and recommends penalties |
| **ADCOM** | Administrative Committee — receives and acts on SPEAM recommendations |
| **SAC** | Students Appeal Committee — handles appeals to the VC for cases beyond 1-year suspension (clemency only for SPEAM outcomes) |

### Appeals for SPEAM Outcomes
- Students must have received a **sanction beyond 1 year** to appeal for clemency
- Must have **served 1 semester** before filing clemency appeal
- File appeal through the **Students Appeal Committee** addressed to the President/Vice-Chancellor within 21 days of written notification
