# Babcock University — Disciplinary Sanctions, Suspension & Dismissal

**Category:** Rules & Regulations
**Subcategory:** Disciplinary Sanctions
**Source:** BU Student Handbook 2021–2024; Final Year Data Document
**Last Updated:** 8th March, 2026
**Format:** Markdown

## Overview

Babcock University operates a tiered disciplinary system. Sanctions range from demerit points (handled by Hall Administrators and Deans Council) to semester suspension, academic-year suspension, and outright dismissal/expulsion for the most serious offences.

> **Note:** The university reserves the right to suspend or dismiss any student in the overall interest of the institution. No legal representations are permitted within the internal administrative disciplinary process.

---

## Tier 1 — Semester Suspension

A student is placed on **semester suspension** and **forfeits all academic work for that semester** if they exhaust their **60 merit points** within a semester.

---

## Tier 2 — Academic Year Suspension (Minimum 1 Year)

A student is suspended for **not less than one academic year** for any of the following:

| Offence |
|---------|
| Using, selling, or possessing narcotics, drugs, alcoholic beverages, tobacco, or other prohibited materials (excluding cocaine — see Tier 3) |
| Gambling of any kind, in any form |
| Sexual immorality — pre/extra-marital sex, kissing, cuddling, caressing, or pregnancy outside wedlock |
| Attending or being found in questionable, prohibited, or unauthorised places |
| Stealing, fraud, or deception (including unauthorised use of another student's electronic card or school fees account) |
| Fighting or other forms of physical violence, including student demonstrations |
| Removing, hiding, or using property (meal tickets, leave cards, exam/financial clearance papers, ID cards, etc.) belonging to the university, staff, or another student without permission |
| Disruption of lectures, examinations, convocations, or university functions |
| Resident students maintaining parallel off-campus accommodation |
| Any unregistered individual loitering and accessing student facilities (hostels, cafeteria, classes, etc.) |
| Forgery — altering, forging, or tampering with university documents, credentials, medical records, or signatures |
| Entering out-of-bound areas, including opposite-sex hostels and toilets |
| Refusing to submit to psycho-social screening or absconding from it |
| Unauthorised video recording, photographing, or sharing of nude images |
| Disruption or disorderliness during/after any university event, including violent birthday celebrations (beating, water-stoning, drink-spraying, tearing of clothes, etc.) |
| Breach of security, safety, or health protocols |
| Hosting or accommodating non-BU students in the halls of residence |

> All suspension sanctions begin running from the **semester in which the infraction was committed**. If a student fails to appear before the SDC when directed, the suspension period starts from the semester the student was directed to appear.

---

## Tier 3 — Automatic Dismissal / Expulsion

A student is liable to **automatic dismissal/expulsion** for any of the following:

| Offence |
|---------|
| Persistent violations of any offences listed under Tier 2 (Suspension B) |
| Gross disrespect to constituted authority |
| Gross academic dishonesty |
| Spreading atheistic or cultic ideas, or actions intended to undermine the university's religious ideals |
| Practising, advocating, soliciting, or teaching same-sex relationships or any sexual orientation contrary to Genesis 2:22–24 |
| Possession of any cult logo, tattoo marks of a known cult group, or inexplicable tattoo |
| Fraternising with or maintaining membership in fraternities, sororities, or secret societies |
| Possessing or using arms or explosives; destroying school property or harming human life |
| Threatening or intimidating with weapons (sharp objects, broken bottles, cutleries, guns, dangerous chemicals, etc.) |
| Gangsterism — a fight or act of violence by more than one person |
| Bullying (unless the university determines a lesser sanction is appropriate given the circumstances) |
| Using, selling, or possessing **cocaine** |

---

## Suspension Commencement Notes

- Suspension starts from the **semester of the infraction**
- If the student fails to appear before the SDC, suspension starts from the **semester of directive to appear**
- All suspended students must **physically leave the university premises** for the duration of their suspension

---

## Readmission After Suspension

Students wishing to return after suspension must:

1. Apply **one month before** the end of the suspension period
2. Submit testimonials from: parent/guardian, employer/supervisor, and counsellor/clergy
3. Upon approval, begin registration through the **Student Support Centre (SSC)**
4. Complete a **3-month Special Rehabilitation Programme** during the first semester of resumption (at own expense)

---

## Expulsion Pardon (VC Discretion)

The President/Vice-Chancellor may pardon an expelled student **at absolute discretion** if:
1. The student has served **at least 2 years** of the sanction
2. In certain special circumstances, the student successfully completes a **1-year Special Rehabilitation Programme** (no academic registration; at own expense)
3. The student demonstrates genuine rehabilitation

---

## Appeals Process

All appeals against outcomes of the **SDC** or **SPEAM** are directed to the **President/Vice-Chancellor** through the **Students Appeal Committee (SAC)**.

| Appeal Type | Deadline | Eligible Parties |
|-------------|----------|-----------------|
| Appeal against decision | Within **21 clear days** of receiving written notice | Any student dissatisfied with SDC or SPEAM outcome |
| Appeal for clemency (SDC outcomes) | Any time | Any student seeking reduced sanction |
| Appeal for clemency (SPEAM outcomes) | After serving **1 semester** of sanction | Only students with sanctions **beyond 1 year** |

### SAC Process
- SAC serves in an **advisory capacity** to the VC
- Students not satisfied with the SAC process may approach external bodies only **after 14 days** from receipt of the decision in writing
- A student who unsuccessfully pursues legal action **may not be re-admitted**
- **Appeal Form** available from the Registry; addressed to: *Secretary, Students Appeal Committee, Babcock University*

---

## Social Media & Internet Conduct

Any conduct on the internet or social media platforms that qualifies as misconduct under Babcock University rules will be treated **as if it occurred on campus** and sanctioned accordingly.

- No legal representations are allowed for students within the university's internal administrative disciplinary process
